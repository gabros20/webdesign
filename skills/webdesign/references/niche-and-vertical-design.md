# Niche & Vertical Design

Purpose: Adapt website structure, proof, and visual register to the expectations of a specific business vertical.

Read when:
- The request names a market, profession, risk profile, or premium-information balance.

Skip when:
- The request is generic craft work with no vertical-specific decision.

Inputs:
- Business model, audience stakes, buying questions, proof assets, and brand posture.

Produces:
- A vertical-specific structure, proof strategy, and premium-information setting.

How to design for a *specific* business, not "a website." The technique references answer *how to build a
distinctive section*; this one answers *what an architect's site needs vs a law firm vs a SaaS* — the
structure, the proof devices, and the visual register each niche's visitors actually respond to.

## The governing idea: the premium↔information dial

A niche site has to satisfy two things at once — **premium brand presence** (be noticed, remembered,
trusted as high-quality) and **high-quality information + proof** (answer questions, prove competence,
reduce risk). These are not a compromise; they're a **dial you set per surface and per scroll-depth**:

- **Premium is signaled by what you leave OUT** — whitespace, restraint, one hero image/idea, slow
  purposeful motion, craft/heritage storytelling instead of review counts. *Luxury whispers.*
- **Proof is signaled by structured DENSITY** — scannable IA, credentials/E-E-A-T, case-study results,
  social proof placed next to the CTA. (Devices: `trust-proof-and-structure.md`.)
- **Sequence them down the page:** an emotional, restrained hero (premium) → progressively denser proof
  and information as the visitor scrolls and self-qualifies → a low-friction action. First viewport earns
  attention; later viewports earn the decision.

**Where you set the dial is decided by stakes & emotion, not taste:** the higher the money/health/legal
risk, the more **information + proof must lead** and the more **conservative** the visual register
(finance, healthcare, law are the most restrained). The more **lifestyle/brand-prestige**-driven (luxury,
high-end real estate), the more **premium/emotional** can lead — but proof always carries equal *final*
weight. Premium polish earns the halo; proof closes the deal. **Never let aesthetics bury the phone
number, the credentials, or the next step.**

## Cross-cutting doctrine

**Premium-creative niches (architecture, interiors, agencies, luxury, photographers/artists):**
- **The work is the interface.** The portfolio/imagery IS the product; the chrome gets out of its way —
  understated UI, neutral canvas, generous whitespace.
- **Premium = restraint, not decoration.** Monochrome/neutral + one accent; 1–2 type families (often a
  refined serif display + clean sans); large editorial photography; slow fade/scroll-reveal motion.
- **Aesthetic is authority, but beauty alone loses the sale** — pair brand presence with proof. Narrative
  arc: **Problem → Empathy → Solution → Proof → Action.**
- **Curate hard** (selectivity is positioning — show the work you want more of). **Real photography only**
  (stock instantly kills perceived value). **Fast load is a premium signal** (47% expect <2s). **Mobile is
  the primary surface.** **Proof appears early.** **The About/Team page converts** (buyers want to know
  *who* they'll work with).

**Trust-heavy niches (law, finance, healthcare, real estate, SaaS, professional services):**
- **Three credibility layers — build all:** **Visual** (design quality, real photography, consistent
  branding — 75% judge credibility on design alone, Stanford) → **Content** (clarity, specificity, plain
  language) → **Social** (reviews, testimonials, named logos, case results — the most powerful, it's
  third-party validation).
- **First screen answers, in ~5–10s:** who you help, what you offer, how to start, what happens next —
  one precise statement + one confidence cue, not broad "excellence."
- **Specificity beats superlatives** — named clients, exact numbers ($ verdicts, AUM, homes sold, % ROI,
  review counts), real photos.
- **Proof adjacent to action** — strongest testimonial/badge/guarantee next to the CTA/form.
- **Friction discipline** — 3–5 form fields, staged intake, risk-reducers ("free," "no obligation"),
  visible contact on every page. **Speed = trust** (sub-3s, ideally sub-1s). **Mobile-first** (60–83%
  of traffic). **Security/compliance visible** (SSL, HIPAA, SOC 2, GDPR) as both function and signal.

## Vertical playbooks

Each: *audience/criteria · goal · structure · proof devices · visual register · dial · pitfalls.*

### Architecture firms (worked example — the canonical premium↔proof case)
- **Audience:** prospective clients (high-consideration; **~9 in 10 pro-services buyers rule a firm out
  before contact, 8+ in 10 evaluate via the website first**) + talent/peers. They need the work AND
  concrete answers: *how you work, timeframe, cost, who I'll work with.* **Design for your ideal client,
  not to impress other architects** (the classic trap).
- **Goal:** get pre-screened-in → book a consult.
- **Structure:** restrained hero (project image + "what we do / who we serve") → positioning/philosophy →
  **portfolio** (curate ~8–25 projects, grouped by type/scale — the spine) → **project case-study pages**
  (brief → scope → challenge → solution → outcome, shown as sketches → site analysis → development → built
  photos: show *process*) → **Team page** (2nd most-visited — headshots, credentials, bios) →
  educational/FAQ content (cost/timeline/process; also SEO) → clear CTAs throughout.
- **Proof:** built-project photography (blueprint → reality); process artifacts (sketches, plans,
  interactive floor plans); awards & press; client testimonials; named scope/role; team credentials.
- **Register:** whitespace framing large architectural photography; type + photo style carry the vibe;
  interactive floor plans / hover / scroll effects used sparingly. **Reflect the brand, don't re-imagine
  the architecture** — let the work stand.
- **Dial:** balanced, tilting to proof — lead visually, but every project answers brief/role/challenge/
  solution and the site answers cost/timeline/process.
- **Pitfalls:** designing for peers not clients; portfolio bloat diluting the best work; gorgeous-but-empty;
  heavy un-optimized images (cap ~2000px / ≤200KB); neglecting mobile (majority of traffic); hiding team/process.

### Interior design / studios
Premium clients buying experience/trust/emotion who want an aesthetic match. **Goal:** attract aligned
high-end inquiries. **Structure:** home → curated portfolio → trust-About → services → testimonials/press →
one strong CTA. **Proof:** large pro project photography (dominant), testimonials, "as seen in." **Register:**
max whitespace, refined serif+sans, restrained palette + one highlight, subtle reveals. **Dial:** heavily
brand/visual-led, anchored by clear services + trust. **Pitfalls:** stock photos (fatal), clutter, over-broad
portfolio muddying the signature look.

### Creative agencies / studios
B2B buyers who decide fast — **if they can't tell what you do in ~5s, they leave.** **Goal:** book a
discovery call. **Structure:** tight 4–6 plain-labeled nav; specific outcome-led value prop above the fold
("we help X brands increase Y by Z%"); social proof in first scrolls; Problem→Empathy→Solution→Proof→Action.
**Proof:** case studies as *decision tools* (challenge → approach → measurable result); client logos;
intent-matched CTAs ("Book a discovery call"). **Register:** clean, fast; expressive motion is on-brand here
(the site is itself a portfolio piece) but never at the cost of clarity/speed. **Dial:** most info/conversion-
weighted of the creative niches. **Pitfalls:** style over substance; clever nav labels; vague value prop;
buried results; slow heavy site.

### Luxury / high-end & hospitality
Affluent buyers expecting five-star digital service; they buy story/transformation/belonging. **Goal:**
communicate exclusivity + convert with zero friction (hospitality: **home → room → booking in ≤3 clicks**,
multi-language/currency). **Structure:** cinematic hero → mood/story → detail (product/room pages) →
frictionless conversion. **Proof:** editorial photography, cinematic video (tours, drone, texture), heritage/
craftsmanship storytelling, consistency across every touchpoint. **Register:** maximum restraint — mono/neutral
palette, gallery whitespace, 1–2 type families, slow motion, every micro-interaction considered; fast despite
heavy media (adaptive video). **Dial:** brand-presence-dominant, but friction-free utility (booking, pricing,
currency) is itself a luxury expectation. **Pitfalls:** hard-sell copy; visual chaos; clashing color; slow
hero media; clunky checkout/booking.

### Photographers / artists
Clients, curators, buyers judging instantly on image quality; buyers need context (**79% want background on
the artist/work before buying**). **Goal:** let the work sell itself → inquiry/commission/sale. **Structure:**
best work top of hierarchy; categorized by series; **About with first-person intro + a photo of the artist's
face**; per-piece context; commerce CTA. **Curation:** photographers ~15–40 (the **20-60-20 rule**: 20% best,
60% solid, 20% range); artists 10–15 pieces, ≤~20/gallery, lightbox viewing. **Proof:** hi-res images,
awards/exhibitions/press, concise captions (concept/materials/context), artist statement. **Register:**
understated UI that never competes; single consistent typeface; whitespace between pieces; minimal effects;
fast galleries. **Dial:** image-dominant but captions/statement are load-bearing for buyers. **Pitfalls:**
over-designed sites stealing focus; uncurated dumps; low-res images; missing context/face; slow galleries.

### Law firms / attorneys
Distressed, high-stakes visitors comparison-shopping on competence + outcomes + trust. **Goal:** book a (free)
consult. **Structure:** hero positioning + visible phone + "Schedule a Free Consultation" on every page above
the fold → one keyword-optimized page **per practice area** (not a generic "Services" list) → attorney profiles
(the "digital handshake") → **case results with specific $ figures/verdicts** → testimonials → resources/blog →
intake. **Proof:** bios with bar admissions/credentials/years; dollar-figure results; rating badges (Super
Lawyers, Avvo, Martindale, Best Lawyers); case-count stats; named/video testimonials; encrypted intake.
**Register:** restrained, authoritative — navy/charcoal/burgundy/muted gold, serif or high-contrast sans, real
attorney/office photography. **Dial:** ~60% info/proof, 40% polish. **Pitfalls:** generic practice pages;
hidden contact; gavel/scales stock clichés; vague "we fight for you"; slow load; non-mobile intake.

### Financial services / wealth / fintech
Affluent, risk-averse, long cycle; decide on trust/stability/fiduciary alignment. **Goal:** schedule an intro
call / resource download. **Structure:** hero positioning + one confidence cue → who-we-serve → **process**
(demystify "how we work") → team with credentials → proof (AUM, tenure, client count) → insights → fiduciary
statement + disclosures → contact. **Proof:** **designations after every name** (CFP/CFA/CPA/ChFC); explicit
**"fiduciary"** language; AUM / year established / avg client tenure / families served; clean SEC/FINRA
disclosures. **Register:** the most conservative — **deep navy + crisp white**, calm palette, abundant
whitespace, restrained type, subtle motion. **Dial:** ~50/50 tilting to proof; over-design erodes trust here
most. **Pitfalls:** vague claims tripping compliance; no specific numbers; missing fiduciary language;
startup-y aesthetics undercutting gravitas; insecure forms.

### Healthcare / medical / dental
Anxious, cautious patients; 70%+ mobile. **Goal:** book appointment + improve inquiry *quality*. **Structure
(4-stage flow):** 1) Fit & Scope (who it's for + what it addresses, answered in ~10s) → 2) Trust & Credibility
(credentials early) → 3) Process Expectations (first-visit details, response timelines) → 4) Action Path
(booking only after clarity). **Proof:** provider credentials/board certs/affiliations early; detailed provider
profiles; outcomes data; patient **video testimonials**; HIPAA-secure forms + privacy cues. **Anxiety
reducers:** state pre-visit expectations near the submit button; explicit response-time commitments; staged
intake (routing-critical fields first). **Register:** calm, reassuring — soft blues/greens/teal + white, warm
real provider/facility photography, high readability. **Dial:** ~65% info/reassurance. **Pitfalls:** aesthetics
over journey clarity; long forms; no "what happens next"; missing credentials; poor mobile.

### Real estate / property
Largest-transaction buyers/sellers/investors; luxury adds lifestyle/prestige; mostly mobile. **Goal:** capture
leads (inquiry/showing/valuation) + keep users in search. **Structure:** hero (brand + search or signature
listing) → property search with **map + listings** → featured listings with galleries/virtual tours → agent
bio + track record → testimonials → buy/sell pages → contact/valuation. *Luxury variant:* emotional narrative
(location → amenities → architecture → floor plans → specs → team) *before* the selection tool. **Proof:**
agent **performance data** (homes sold, volume, days-on-market, sale-to-list); testimonials with names +
property photos; awards/affiliation; for developments — construction progress/live-cam, architect video,
design awards. **Register:** premium = minimalism; cinematic architectural photography/renders; categorized
galleries; restrained motion. **Dial:** luxury ~60% premium/emotional; mainstream tilts to search utility +
social proof. **Pitfalls:** clutter OR sterile over-minimalism; stock imagery; weak track-record data; slow
galleries; search friction.

### B2B SaaS / tech products
A **buying committee** (technical evaluator, finance/ROI, executive); the 5-second test rules. **Goal:** demo
request or trial signup. **Structure (ordered):** hero (outcome headline <8 words, sub-head with a proof point,
**product UI screenshot/visual demo**, primary CTA, trust bar) → problem→solution → **multi-stakeholder value**
(ROI calculator / tech specs / implementation timeline / comparison) → social proof + case studies →
feature→benefit (show transformation) → product video → secondary CTA → FAQ. **Proof:** **named customer logos**,
G2 ratings, usage metrics ("Join 5,000+ companies"), quantified case outcomes, **security/compliance badges
(SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, WCAG AA)**. **CTA:** one primary per page; first-person ("Start My Free Trial");
risk-reducers ("No Credit Card Required"); 3–5 form fields; offer trial + demo for different readiness.
**Register:** product-forward — real screenshots/silent loops, workflow micro-animations, WCAG AA, WebP/AVIF +
lazy-load, distinct mobile layouts. **Dial:** ~55% info/proof. **Pitfalls:** jargon failing the 5-sec test;
capability lists not outcomes; one stakeholder addressed; generic trust bars; long forms; competing CTAs.

### Professional services / consulting / local high-ticket (contractors, accountants)
High-ticket with information asymmetry → they judge *signals* (75% judge credibility on design — the **halo
effect**: premium site → assumed expertise). **Goal:** book a discovery/consult call. **Structure (5 pages):**
Home (value + CTA) → Services → **Case Studies** → trust-About → Contact; content-first/authority approach
(lead with insights). **Proof:** *consultants/accountants* — **membership/cert badges on every page** (ICAEW,
ACCA, CPA, Xero/QuickBooks partner), **Google reviews (25+ @ 4.5★+)**, quantified case studies (visible
credentials + social proof can lift conversion up to ~200%); *contractors/local* — **before/after photos,
finished-project galleries, real jobsite imagery**, licenses/insurance/warranties/financing, service-area
pages. **Register:** premium-but-approachable (the halo) — clean design, real team/project photography,
confident type. **Dial:** ~50/50 — premium polish *is* a primary trust signal here, but proof must carry equal
weight. **Pitfalls:** cheap/DIY look (kills the halo); no cases/photos; hidden credentials; vague scope; weak
mobile; no clear next step / service-area clarity.

## Where each vertical sits on the brand↔info axis

| Vertical | Brand-presence weight | Info/proof weight | Signature proof device |
|---|---|---|---|
| Luxury / hospitality | Highest | Friction-free utility | Cinematic video + heritage story |
| Interior design | High | Moderate | Large editorial project photography |
| Real estate (luxury) | High | Moderate–high | Cinematic renders + track-record data |
| Photographers / artists | High (visual) | Context-critical for buyers | Hi-res work + statement/captions |
| Architecture | Balanced | High (process + answers) | Process-driven project case studies |
| Professional services | Balanced (halo) | Balanced | Case studies + credentials/reviews |
| Law | Info-led | High | $-figure case results + rating badges |
| B2B SaaS | Info-led | Highest | Named logos + metric case studies + security |
| Healthcare | Info-led | Highest (reassurance) | Credentials + patient video + process clarity |
| Finance / wealth | Most conservative | Highest (disclosure) | Designations + AUM/tenure + fiduciary |

## How to use this reference
1. **Name the vertical** (and the *ideal client* within it — design for them, not peers).
2. **Set the dial** from the table — how much premium-lead vs proof-lead, and how conservative the register.
3. **Lay out the structure** from that vertical's playbook; pick the **signature proof device** for the niche.
4. **Sequence** premium hero → densifying proof → low-friction action.
5. Build each section with the technique references; pull the proof/trust mechanics from
   `trust-proof-and-structure.md` and the persuasion/conversion mechanics from `persuasion-and-conversion.md`.
